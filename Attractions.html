Attractions

Waterford, despite its small size, has a healthy amount of attractions, making it an extremely interesting city to visit and live in. The first of these is Reginald's tower, an ancient tower built in 917 and the only artefact in Ireland to be named directly after a real-life Viking. This tower is located in the Viking triangle and is free to enter for the public, in addition to being accompanied by a 40-foot sculpture of a Viking sword located just behind it, the world reccord holder for the larges sculpture of a sword.

Waterford Crystal is a stunning experience, showing Waterford's long history in the glass sculpting industry. It also details the process of sculpting with glass in the modern age and shows off a great number of exquisite glass sculptures including glass harps, teddy bears and grandfather clocks.

Waterford also includes a hiking track, known as the Waterford greenway. This track runs all the way from Waterford to Dungarvan, and features its own miniature locomotive which runs the area. In addition to being a stunningly gorgeous walk, the greenway also hosts a large Viking Reenactment every August, showing off how the Vikings lived and fought.

Waterford also sports an exciting nightlife, to the excitement of most students. Waterford is home to a large number of pubs, discos and clubs, the Ruby Lounge on Main Street being one of it's best examples.
