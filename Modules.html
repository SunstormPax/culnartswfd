Modules

With the exception of third year which is spent on Industrial Work Placement or International Studies, each semester of the Culinary Arts Course contains between 4 and 5 different modules, at least 1 of which is always elective. These modules change every semester, allowing the course to stay fresh.

The first semester will include an introduction to choosing and cooking food, food safety, a break-down of fundamental culinary skills and lessons on how to think critically and react to any situation. There will also be a choice between learning about language or about business

Semester 2 will continue teaching culinary skills, this time in more detail. It will also focus on the scientific aspects of nutrition and on how food relates to tourism. Students can also choose between continuing to study language or studying organisation and behaviour.

Semester 3 continues culinary skills, this time in much more advanced detail, as well as focusing on both food analysis and financial accounting and how it relates to business. This semester has three choices for it's elective modules, those being entrepreneurship, marketing, or continuing on with language.

Semester 4 shifts focus towards Microbiology as well as detailing cost and budgeting operations and discussing sustainability practices regarding food and energy. Students can also choose between studying Research skills or language for a final time.

Skipping on to fourth year, Semester 7 teaches about food policies as well as two modules the student can choose. The first choice is between studying Classic and contemporary cuisine, advanced culinary applications in a general scope, and advanced culinary applications focused on pastry and confectionary. The second choice is between food innovation and food journalism.

The final semester focuses on European food regulations and allows the student to continue studying the electives they chose from the previous course in greater detail.
